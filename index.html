About Me

I am a PhD student since 2018 at the Courant Institute, New York University, working with Thomas Wies.

I am broadly interested in rigorous analysis and verification of concurrent data structures. My thesis specifically focuses on wide range of concurrent search structure implementations (i.e key/value stores). My work encompasses both automated and manual approaches to verification, and advances the current state of the art.

Beyond concurrent data structures, I have also worked on program synthesis for robot systems. For future, I am looking to incorporate problems from distributed systems and program synthesis into my research.
